Visit Underground River

The Underground River in Palawan, also known as the Puerto Princesa Underground River, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of Nature. It is located in the Saint Paul Mountain Range on the island of Palawan, in the Philippines. The Underground River is a subterranean river that flows out to the South China Sea. It is approximately 8.2 kilometers long and is considered one of the longest underground rivers in the world. It runs through a spectacular limestone karst landscape, featuring caves and caverns filled with stunning rock formations, as well as several chambers and large halls.

It is home to a diverse array of flora and fauna, including a number of species that are found only in this location. The river is home to more than 800 plant species and different bat species, and monitor lizards. The area is also home to monkeys and swiftlets whose nests are harvested to make bird's nest soup. Access to the Underground River is limited to guided boat tours that take visitors through the first 2 kilometers of the river. These tours are available daily and depart from Sabang, a small town located a few kilometers from the park. To visit the river, visitor need to have a permit from the Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park Office, and It is also closed on certain days for maintenance. The Underground River is an incredible natural wonder and a must-see for visitors to Palawan. It is not only a major tourism attraction but also an ecological treasure, that the Philippine Government and UNESCO are trying to protect and preserve.
