Visit Tabon Caves

The Tabon Caves are a series of caves located on the island of Palawan in the Philippines. They are located in the Tabon Cave Complex, a National Cultural Treasure, which covers an area of 52 hectares on the western coast of the island, near the town of Quezon. The Tabon Caves are known for their rich history and cultural significance, as they were the site of some of the earliest human habitation in the Philippines. The caves have been used by humans for over 50,000 years and have been the subject of several archaeological excavations.

The Tabon Caves are a complex of about 29 limestone caves, of which, the Tabon Cave proper is the most important as it contains the Tabon Man, the oldest human remains found in the Philippines, dating back to about 22,000 to 24,000 years ago. The Tabon Cave Complex is also considered as an important archaeological site, as it has yielded important cultural artifacts, including tools and pottery, from the Tabon Cave Man. Visitors can tour the Tabon Cave Complex, which includes a museum that displays the artifacts and human remains found in the caves, as well as guided tours of the caves themselves. The tours take visitors through the main cave, which features large chambers with impressive rock formations and subterranean ponds. It's a rare chance to explore the ancient history, archaeology and anthropology of the Philippines, the Tabon Caves are a must-see destination for history buffs and those interested in the Philippines' rich cultural heritage.
